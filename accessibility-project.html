delve into accessibility

The assignment

Accessibility is a subject I didn’t put too much focus on during my studies. But I did know it is something that’s becoming more and more important over time. During my internship I got the question to give a presentation about this subject. My immediate reaction was yes, but I told them: “Give me some time to delve into this subject and I’ll do it!” By following a masterclass from the Accessibility foundation, interviewing visually impaired people and a visit to the muZIEum museum in Nijmegen I dove into accessibility. During my dive into this subject, I focused on visual limitations. I didn’t have the time to touch other fields within this subject.

My research

Masterclass Accessibility Foundation

The masterclass was divided into two evenings. There, I learned about the fundamentals of accessibility in general and on the web. The first evening of the masterclass consisted of listening to information and asking questions to the expert. This already provided a lot of insight. On the second evening, we got hands-on experience evaluating a website. I learned how to examine a website to analyze it.

interviewing visually impaired people

After the masterclass, I wanted to learn more. That's why I decided that I wanted to conduct interviews with several visually impaired individuals. Through my network, I found four people who were willing to assist me. I spoke with them and immediately noticed that a visual impairment brings more challenges than I initially thought. It provided me with a clear insight into how the online world is still not fully navigable for blind and visually impaired individuals.

visiting the MuZIEum in Nijmegen

In my opinion, the MuZIEum is a must-visit in Nijmegen. The dark experience I had there truly allowed me to understand what it's like to be blind. The friendly guide was able to personally assist us with any questions we had. They shared their own experiences, which were confirmed by the experience itself at the same time. The dark experience involved walking through dark rooms with certain setups using a guide stick. I don't want to give away too much about how it felt, but after this experience, I gained a clear understanding of the daily life of someone with visual impairments. After the dark experience, I wrote a postcard in braille. That’s what is illustrated in the picture.

my proces

Masterclass Accessibility Foundation

This project really became my project. From the moment I was asked to give a presentation, I took it seriously. By showing a lot of interest, I learned a ton.

Interviewing visually impaired people

Speaking to visually impaired individuals was very enlightening. They shared their experiences, and it positively impacted me. Overall, the people I spoke to have a positive outlook on life, so that I can learn something from them. Their stories made me curious about how I would experience being visually impaired myself.

MuZIEum in Nijmegen

That's how I ended up at the MuZIEum in Nijmegen, where I had a dark experience that truly opened my eyes (pun intended). After the experience, I had an even better understanding of what it's like to not be able to see (well).

The presentation

I gave the presentation to the product team, in which I also worked, at my internship company. The presentation started by obtaining empathy for the visually impaired. I did this by introducing them to the visual impairment Séverine, the lady who gave the first part of the masterclass. Additionally, I had invited one of the interviewed visually limited persons to support me. In the second part, I incorporated the company's core values into the topic. I let the audience experience what it's like to navigate the Volkskrant app with a screen reader without actually seeing what is happening. After they did this blindly, I showed the original video. At the end, I provided tips on how to further improve it.
